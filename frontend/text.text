# VITAMINS

## Introduction

Vitamins are organic compounds required by the body in very small quantities for normal growth, metabolism, immunity and proper functioning of organs. Although vitamins are needed in small amounts, their deficiency can lead to serious diseases.

Vitamins are divided into two groups:

Fat-soluble vitamins:
Vitamin A, Vitamin D, Vitamin E and Vitamin K.

Water-soluble vitamins:
Vitamin B Complex and Vitamin C.

Fat-soluble vitamins can be stored in the body, whereas water-soluble vitamins are generally not stored and excess amounts are excreted through urine.

---

## Vitamin A (Retinol)

Vitamin A is important for vision, healthy skin, immunity and normal growth.

Major sources include carrots, papaya, mango, milk, butter, egg yolk and green leafy vegetables.

Deficiency of Vitamin A causes Night Blindness. In severe deficiency, Xerophthalmia develops, which causes dryness of the eye and may lead to blindness.

Night Blindness is one of the most frequently asked deficiency diseases in SSC examinations.

---

## Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)

Vitamin B1 helps in carbohydrate metabolism and proper functioning of nerves and muscles.

Sources include whole grains, pulses, nuts and cereals.

Deficiency of Vitamin B1 causes Beriberi.

Symptoms of Beriberi include muscle weakness, nerve disorders and difficulty in movement.

---

## Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)

Vitamin B2 helps in energy production and maintenance of healthy skin and eyes.

Milk, eggs and green vegetables are important sources.

Deficiency causes Cheilosis, characterized by cracks at the corners of the mouth.

---

## Vitamin B3 (Niacin)

Vitamin B3 is necessary for metabolism and healthy functioning of the digestive and nervous systems.

Deficiency causes Pellagra.

Pellagra is characterized by the three D's:

Dermatitis

Diarrhea

Dementia

---

## Vitamin B9 (Folic Acid)

Folic acid is essential for formation of red blood cells and proper growth.

Deficiency can cause anemia and developmental problems.

---

## Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Vitamin B12 is necessary for red blood cell formation and proper functioning of the nervous system.

Deficiency causes Pernicious Anemia.

Questions on Vitamin B12 and anemia are commonly asked in SSC exams.

---

## Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

Vitamin C plays an important role in immunity, wound healing and maintenance of connective tissues.

Important sources are Amla, Orange, Lemon and Guava.

Deficiency causes Scurvy.

Symptoms include bleeding gums, weak bones and delayed wound healing.

Vitamin C is also known as Ascorbic Acid, a very common SSC question.

---

## Vitamin D (Calciferol)

Vitamin D helps in absorption of calcium and phosphorus and is essential for healthy bones and teeth.

The most important source is sunlight.

Other sources include fish, egg yolk and dairy products.

Deficiency in children causes Rickets.

Deficiency in adults causes Osteomalacia.

Rickets is one of the most repeated questions in SSC examinations.

---

## Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

Vitamin E acts as an antioxidant and helps protect body cells from damage.

It is important for reproductive health and muscle function.

Sources include vegetable oils, nuts and seeds.

---

## Vitamin K

Vitamin K is necessary for blood clotting.

Deficiency results in excessive bleeding because blood clotting becomes slow.

Green leafy vegetables are the major source.

Questions on blood clotting and Vitamin K are frequently asked.

---

## Mineral Deficiencies

Iron is required for the formation of hemoglobin.

Iron deficiency causes Anemia.

Symptoms include weakness, fatigue and pale skin.

Iodine is necessary for the production of thyroid hormones.

Deficiency of iodine causes Goitre, characterized by swelling of the thyroid gland in the neck.

Calcium is essential for bones and teeth.

Its deficiency can weaken bones and increase the risk of fractures.

---

## Protein Energy Malnutrition

Kwashiorkor occurs due to severe protein deficiency.

Children suffering from Kwashiorkor develop swollen abdomen, stunted growth and muscle wasting.

Marasmus occurs due to deficiency of both proteins and calories.

Affected children become extremely thin and weak.

---

## Important Deficiency Diseases

Night Blindness is caused by deficiency of Vitamin A.

Beriberi is caused by deficiency of Vitamin B1.

Pellagra is caused by deficiency of Vitamin B3.

Pernicious Anemia is caused by deficiency of Vitamin B12.

Scurvy is caused by deficiency of Vitamin C.

Rickets is caused by deficiency of Vitamin D.

Goitre is caused by deficiency of Iodine.

Anemia is caused by deficiency of Iron.

Kwashiorkor is caused by protein deficiency.

Marasmus is caused by protein and calorie deficiency.

---

# HUMAN DISEASES

## Introduction

A disease is a condition in which the normal functioning of the body is disturbed. Diseases may affect a particular organ, a body system, or the entire body. They can be caused by microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protozoa, or by nutritional deficiencies, genetic disorders, and lifestyle factors.

The study of diseases is called Pathology.

An organism that causes disease is called a Pathogen.

An organism that carries disease-causing pathogens from one person to another is called a Vector.

Examples:

Mosquito → Malaria, Dengue

Housefly → Cholera, Typhoid

Sand Fly → Kala-azar

---

## Classification of Diseases

Diseases can be classified into two broad categories:

### Communicable Diseases

These diseases spread from one person to another through air, water, food, physical contact, blood, insects, or other carriers.

Examples include Tuberculosis, Cholera, Typhoid, AIDS, Dengue and Malaria.

### Non-Communicable Diseases

These diseases do not spread from one person to another.

Examples include Diabetes, Cancer, Hypertension, Goitre and Deficiency Diseases.

---

# Diseases Caused by Protozoa

Protozoa are microscopic single-celled organisms.

---

## Malaria

Malaria is one of the most important diseases asked in competitive examinations.

It is caused by a protozoan parasite called Plasmodium.

The disease is transmitted by the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.

When the mosquito bites a person, Plasmodium enters the bloodstream and initially affects the liver. It later attacks red blood cells.

Symptoms include:

High fever

Chills

Sweating

Headache

Body pain

Important Facts:

Malaria is caused by Plasmodium.

Female Anopheles mosquito is the vector.

It affects liver and RBCs.

---

## Amoebiasis

Amoebiasis is caused by Entamoeba histolytica.

The infection spreads through contaminated food and water.

It mainly affects the intestine and causes severe diarrhea.

---

## Kala-Azar

Kala-Azar is caused by Leishmania donovani.

The disease is transmitted by the bite of a Sand Fly.

It affects the liver, spleen and bone marrow.

Kala-Azar is also known as Black Fever.

---

## Sleeping Sickness

Sleeping Sickness is caused by Trypanosoma.

It is spread by the Tsetse Fly.

The disease affects the nervous system and causes abnormal sleeping patterns.

---

# Diseases Caused by Bacteria

Bacteria are microscopic organisms that can cause several infectious diseases.

---

## Tuberculosis (TB)

Tuberculosis is caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

The disease mainly affects the lungs but may also affect bones, kidneys and other organs.

Symptoms include:

Persistent cough

Blood in sputum

Weight loss

Night sweating

Mild fever

The vaccine used against TB is BCG.

Important Facts:

TB is a bacterial disease.

BCG vaccine provides protection.

The lungs are the primary organ affected.

---

## Typhoid

Typhoid is caused by Salmonella typhi.

It spreads through contaminated food and water.

Symptoms include:

High fever

Weakness

Abdominal pain

Loss of appetite

Typhoid is commonly associated with poor sanitation.

---

## Cholera

Cholera is caused by Vibrio cholerae.

The disease spreads through contaminated water and food.

Symptoms include:

Severe diarrhea

Vomiting

Dehydration

Loss of body fluids

Cholera is considered a water-borne disease.

---

## Pneumonia

Pneumonia is commonly caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae.

It affects the lungs and causes inflammation of air sacs.

Symptoms include:

Chest pain

Fever

Difficulty in breathing

Cough

---

## Diphtheria

Diphtheria is caused by Corynebacterium diphtheriae.

It mainly affects the throat and respiratory tract.

A thick membrane develops in the throat, making breathing difficult.

---

## Leprosy

Leprosy is caused by Mycobacterium leprae.

It affects skin and peripheral nerves.

Leprosy is also known as Hansen's Disease.

---

# Diseases Caused by Viruses

Viruses are extremely small infectious agents that reproduce only inside living cells.

---

## AIDS

AIDS stands for Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome.

It is caused by Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV).

The virus attacks white blood cells, especially lymphocytes, weakening the immune system.

Modes of Transmission:

Infected blood transfusion

Unprotected sexual contact

Mother to child

Use of infected needles

AIDS does not spread through touching, sharing food or shaking hands.

Important Fact:

HIV attacks the immune system.

---

## Dengue

Dengue is caused by Dengue Virus.

It spreads through the bite of Aedes aegypti mosquito.

Symptoms include:

High fever

Joint pain

Muscle pain

Headache

Decrease in platelet count

The Aedes mosquito usually bites during daytime.

---

## Chikungunya

Chikungunya is caused by Chikungunya Virus.

The vector is also Aedes mosquito.

The disease is characterized by severe joint pain and fever.

---

## Rabies

Rabies is caused by Rabies Virus.

It spreads through bites of infected animals such as dogs, cats and monkeys.

The virus affects the brain and nervous system.

If untreated, rabies is almost always fatal.

---

## Polio

Polio is caused by Poliovirus.

It affects the nervous system and may cause permanent paralysis.

The Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) is used for prevention.

India was declared Polio-Free in 2014.

---

## Hepatitis

Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver caused by different viruses.

Types include:

Hepatitis A

Hepatitis B

Hepatitis C

Hepatitis D

Hepatitis E

The disease primarily affects the liver.

Common symptoms include jaundice, weakness and loss of appetite.

---

## Measles

Measles is a highly contagious viral disease.

Symptoms include:

Fever

Skin rash

Cough

Runny nose

---

## Chickenpox

Chickenpox is caused by Varicella Virus.

Symptoms include:

Itchy red blisters

Fever

Weakness

---

# Diseases Caused by Fungi

Fungi are organisms that grow in warm and moist conditions.

---

## Ringworm

Ringworm is a fungal disease of the skin.

Despite its name, it is not caused by a worm.

It causes circular itchy patches on the skin.

---

## Athlete's Foot

Athlete's Foot is a fungal infection affecting the feet, especially between the toes.

---

# Deficiency Diseases

Deficiency diseases occur due to lack of nutrients in the diet.

---

## Anemia

Anemia occurs due to deficiency of Iron.

The level of hemoglobin decreases.

Symptoms include:

Weakness

Fatigue

Dizziness

Pale skin

---

## Goitre

Goitre occurs due to deficiency of Iodine.

The thyroid gland enlarges, causing swelling in the neck.

---

## Night Blindness

Night Blindness is caused by deficiency of Vitamin A.

Affected individuals cannot see properly in dim light.

---

## Scurvy

Scurvy occurs due to deficiency of Vitamin C.

Symptoms include:

Bleeding gums

Weak bones

Delayed wound healing

---

## Rickets

Rickets occurs in children due to deficiency of Vitamin D.

The bones become soft and weak.

---

## Osteomalacia

Osteomalacia occurs in adults due to deficiency of Vitamin D.

---

## Beriberi

Beriberi occurs due to deficiency of Vitamin B1.

It affects the nervous and muscular systems.

---

## Pellagra

Pellagra occurs due to deficiency of Vitamin B3.

The characteristic symptoms are:

Dermatitis

Diarrhea

Dementia

---

## Kwashiorkor

Kwashiorkor occurs due to protein deficiency.

Symptoms include:

Swollen abdomen

Poor growth

Muscle wasting

---

## Marasmus

Marasmus occurs due to deficiency of both proteins and calories.

The child becomes extremely thin and weak.

---

# Vaccines and Immunization

Vaccination stimulates the immune system to produce protection against diseases.

Important Vaccines:

BCG → Tuberculosis

OPV → Polio

DPT → Diphtheria, Pertussis and Tetanus

MMR → Measles, Mumps and Rubella

Hepatitis B Vaccine → Hepatitis B

---

Aedes Mosquito spreads Dengue and Chikungunya.

Culex Mosquito spreads Filariasis.

Sand Fly spreads Kala-Azar.

House Fly spreads Cholera and Typhoid by contaminating food.

Tsetse Fly spreads Sleeping Sickness.
